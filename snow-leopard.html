Snow Leopard

Snow leopards live in the mountains of central Asia and the Indian subcontinent with elevations of around 6,000 feet during the winter and around 18,000 feet in the summer. They are mammals are are in the Panthera genus along with lions and tigers. Most snow leopards weight between 60 to 120 pounds, have a height of two feet, and a length of four to five feet. This makes them one of the smaller species of big cats. In their habitat, snow leopards are not hunted by other animals, but they are hunted by humans which have caused them to become a vunerable species.
