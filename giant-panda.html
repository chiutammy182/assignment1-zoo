Giant Panda

The giant panda is a species that is native of China. The average lifespan of a giant panda is 20 years in the wild. This species is vunerable of extinction with roughly 1,864 giant pandas in their native habitats and 600 reside in zoos or breeding centers. Giant pandas have lived for millions of years in bamboo forests. Because of their thick and wooly coat of fur, they are able to stay warm in their habitat. They have inefficient digestion so they must consume an abnormally large amount of bamboo; 70 -100 pounds of bamboo per day.
